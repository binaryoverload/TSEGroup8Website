Data Conclusions

The original aim for this project was to research a correlation between the rate of COVID-19 cases presenting in the UK and the number of flights entering the UK. It has since come to light that, due to the number of factors that will have affected the rate of COVID-19 cases, it is incredibly difficult to find a causational relationship between the two statistics. Due to a number of limitations in the datasets used, this is made further difficult.

Dataset Limitations

The datasets this project used were public datasets that have been published for general use. Because of this, the project is prevented from a direct causational analysis between the two datasets.

COVID-19 Dataset

The COVID-19 dataset is available publicly at the time of writing from the UK governments data dashboard. When downloading the dataset, an option of the different metrics of data you would like to download is given. For the project, 2 metrics were chosen:

  • cumCasesBySpecimenDate - The number of cumulative cases since the start of the dataset
  • newCasesBySpecimenDate - The number of news cases on the record's day

One of the ways a limitation was avoided with the Coronavirus dataset was to ensure both metrics were using "BySpecimenDate" which meant the data was more representative of when test subjects presented with the virus rather than when the test was completed.

The key limitation in the dataset is the large variation in testing throughout the whole dataset period. There is no way to verify that an even proportion of the UK was being testing at the time nor whether there were enough tests available for people. Because of this, it's likely the real Coronavirus numbers vary from our dataset.
